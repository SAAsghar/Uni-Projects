Structured Approach

A methodical approach to software development and implementation

The structured approach follows a very rigid adherence to the software development cycle. The completed software program is designed and implemented one step at a time prior to completion. This approach is best suited for designing projects of a large scale where performance and reliability are vital requirements. Oftenly when there are numerous requirments and mass amounts of data being collected, a reliable implementation of software is required. This approach will aim to resolve an issue and design new software by completing each step of the software development cycle; definning, planning, designing, implementing, testing, maintenance, prior to completion to create 'the perfect prpgram'.

This approach is mostly used when there is a large budget, a long period of time to complete the project, multiple requirements, a large-scale project involved and when professional teams of programmers are required.

Advantages of using this approach are:

- Professional programmers are involved

- Reliable end result

- Methodical and fixed order of tasks

Disadvantages of using this approach are:

- Expensive

- Long period of time required to complete

- Requirements must be well known and understood prior to starting
