Prototyping Approach

Creates multiple versions which are never released, except for the final product

The prototyping approach can be thought of as similar to the agile approach as it too releases multiple versions, however, none of those versions are ever released as they are considered as only concepts or prototypes leading to the final product which is released; hence the name. This approach is best suited when the requirements are not yet known or fully understood and for small to medium scaled projects which are not too complex. The developer creates a concept which is then shown to the user whom provides feedback. This approach relies heavily on user feedback to make appropriate changes within each iteration to satisfy their new and old requirments.

The software being developed goes through continual refinement until the user is satisfied with the product and believes it satisfies all of their requirements developed throughout development. Due to the process of constant updating prior to release, the prototyping approach is easily modified to adapt to new changes and requirements.

Advantages of using this approach are:

- Easily modified

- Low budget

- Adaptable to changes

- Professional programmers involved

- Good for small to medium sized projects

Disadvantages of using this approach are:

- Relies heavily on user feedback

- Creates many versions which are not released except for the finsihed one

- No fast releases due to the constant chnages required by the user; slowest approach of the 5

- Can be tedious or annoying by constant requests for change by the user
