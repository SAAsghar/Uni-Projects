End-User Approach

Budget program development for the everyday user

The End-User approach is the cheapest and easiest approach of the 5 as it is carried out by the end-user at their own expense based on their own range of skills using off-the-shelf products such as, Microsoft Access and Excel, Visual Basic, and Adobe Dreamweaver. The end-user is the developer and maintainer of their own program which is usually intended for usage by selected staff members and others working within the organisation and is designed to last many years.

The development process folows no set order of tasks prior to completion; lacks formal stages, and is easily modified throughout usually with no documentation following, unlike other approaches.

This approach is best suited for small scaled projects with a low budget and short time period. The user must understand their requirements and how to implement them into their own program in order to develop the finished product to use within their organisation.

Advantages of this approach:

- Easily modified

- No or little documentation included

- low budget

- Relatively quick development time

 Disadvantages:

- Requires an understanding of the software

- Takes longer to develop

- Large consideration towards the end-user skill level
